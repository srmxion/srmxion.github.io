About The Robotics Club

The Robotics Club of SRM University, Vadapalani Campus, was born on the 8th of March 2011, as a joint effort between students and teachers to build a platform where they could channel and give form to their passion for Robotics. It kicked off to a roaring start with the organizing of the two day workshop on BASCOM based robotics design in March 2011 and the workshop on Robot design using Arduino Microcontroller in August 2011. March 2012 was eventful for the club with the conception of Xion’12, a two day national level technical symposium comprising robot competitions, technical and fun events followed by a workshop on Sixth sense robotics with vision robotics. In March 2013, the club ventured into PIC Microcontroller based robotic design by hosting a two day workshop. The year of 2014 saw three workshops being conducted, Accelerobotics-gesture controlled robots and Biped-walking robot in March 2014 followed by ISensoBots-sensors based multi-application robot design in August 2014. The same year also saw the much awaited Xion’14 make an appearance in March with exciting technical and fun events making quite a splash on all the enthusiastic participants who came in large numbers. Emboldened with the success of Xion’14, the Robotics Club now brings to you, Xion’15.

GOBLIN BOTS

  • Rules
  • Judging
  • Contact

General Rules
  • The BOT should run from the starting point of the arena
  • Any damage inflicted upon the arena by the BOT will lead to the disqualification of the team

Round 1 - Day 1

  • Tougher Arena

  • Participants can also employ a cooling mechanism to keep their electronics cool.
  • Judging will be strict and the participant will be immediately disqualified in case their car is found to violate any of the above machine specifications.

Grab-Bots

The goal of the contest is to design a 3-DoF Robotic Arm that will pick & place objects (cubes) within its workspace as fast as possible.

  • Arena
  • Specs
  • Rules
  • Contact

  • The Arena will be divided into two sections, in one section/one end the stationary robotic arm will be placed & in the other section/other end a conveyor belt for carrying the cubes will be present.
  • There will be two rounds.
  • In the first round, one of the organizers will place one cube at a time on the movable conveyor belt in random positions within the workspace/reachable area of the robotic arm which will be controlled by one of the team member.
  • Top four performers of the first round will be eligible for round two in which the team members are supposed to place the cubes on the marked spots of the conveyor belt while it is moving.
  • Dragging of the cube is not allowed, the robotic arm has to completely pick/lift the cube on air & drop it in either side of the conveyor belt or the cube stand.
  • The cubes are made up of thermocols covered with colored velvet paper for better gripping.
  • The weight of a cube is less than 50gms & one side is 7cm
  • The robotic arm should not have less than 3DoF & should mimic yaw-pitch-roll rotations
  • The robotic arm should have its power on board i.e. the power source should be on the machine itself and be a non-combustible power source of max 18 Volt DC.
  • The control mechanism of the robotic arm can be either autonomous or anything except wired remote (DPDT switches types)
  • However wireless remote control is allowed & if you are using wireless control – interferences may be there and are unavoidable, it must be compensated for by the robotic arm itself.
  • It is not compulsory to use the same robotic arm which is provided to you during the workshops/courses you had attended.
  • You are allowed to modify your robotic arm (increase RPM of motor, no. & type of motor, no. & type of sensors, control mechanism, no. of batteries & its voltage/current rating, chassis design etc.), to increase your chances of winning, but make sure that the changes are not violating any of the rules specified for this competition.
  • The robotic arm should not damage the game field and should not leave any parts, components of it or a trail behind to mark its path.
  • Per team only one robotic arm is allowed.
  • Team members are not allowed to touch the robotic arm once the game begins.
  • Loose connections, malfunctioning of the haptic suit, touch screen, any other control mechanism, robotic arm etc are unavoidable and need to compensate for by the Robotic arm/team itself.
  • Any robotic arm needs any adjustments like tightening the screw/nut-bolt, fixing of loose connections will be allowed for a reattempt. The robot must start the course again from the beginning, and a maximum of 1 reattempt is allowed.
  • Only one team member is allowed for a maximum of 3 times to touch the robotic arm within their time limit to do any kind of adjustment or removing wires if twisted on the robotic arm.
  • During reattempt you are not allowed to change the microcontroller board, program, power supply, and chassis & control mechanism of the robotic arm
  • Time duration for each team/the competition will be declared on-the-spot by looking into the crowd.
  • Time shall be recorded by a judge with a stopwatch.
  • Time will be recorded from the moment the judge says “start” until the time the judge says “stop” looking into the stop watch calibrated as per the time duration of the competition.
  • No sample arena/test run will be given to any team, however 4-5 sample cubes for all the teams will be provided at the venue for testing and practicing purpose.
  • Failure to comply with any of the above rules will lead to disqualification by the organizers.
  • The decisions of all officials regarding these rules and the conduct of the event shall be final.
  • Round one winners will be judged on the basis of maximum number of cubes picked up in limited time
  • Round two winner will be judged on the basis of maximum number of cubes placed in limited time.

Haptics

Dates : 17th & 18th March 2015
Cost : Rs. 1350 per head

Haptics is the science of applying touch sensation and control for interaction with virtual or physical applications.
